# SutrVerse — Founding Engineer: Technical Assignment

## Build a Salon Management App

Build a web application that helps salons manage their day-to-day operations — stylists, appointments, walk-ins, and business overview.

---

## The Scenario

A salon owner wants to bring their business online. They need a system where:

### Salon Owner
- Adds and manages **stylists** (name, specialties, profile photo)
- Sets **operating hours** for the salon (days of the week, open/close times)
- Defines **available time slots** per stylist (e.g., 30-min or 1-hour slots)
- Sees an **overall business dashboard** — today's bookings, revenue summary, stylist utilization, upcoming appointments

### Stylist
- Views their **personal schedule** — upcoming bookings, free slots
- Manages **walk-in customers** — quickly logs a walk-in with service details
- Sees their **booking history**

### Customer (Public-Facing)
- Visits a **booking website** (no login required)
- Browses **available stylists** with their specialties
- Checks **real-time availability** and picks a slot
- **Books an appointment** — provides name, phone number, selects service
- Receives a **booking confirmation** (on-screen; email/SMS is a bonus)

### System
- Prevents **double bookings** — once a slot is booked, it's unavailable
- Handles **time-zone-aware** scheduling (if applicable)
- Works across **mobile and desktop** browsers

## Technical Guidelines

- **Stack**: Use whatever you're comfortable with. We use Next.js, React, TypeScript, PostgreSQL — if you want to match our stack, great. If you prefer something else, that's fine too. Good architecture transcends frameworks.
- **Deployment**: Deploy it somewhere publicly accessible — Vercel, Railway, Netlify, Render, wherever.
- **Time**: We suggest **2-3 focused days**. If you're AI-native, you'll ship fast. Don't over-engineer — ship the MVP, document the rest.
- **Database**: Use a real database (PostgreSQL, MySQL, SQLite). No in-memory-only solutions.
